# Task: warm-up inferences before the audio callback stabilize inference engine runtimes

## Source Context

From [[anira-2024]] (Ackva & Schulz, TU Berlin, arXiv:2506.12665v1):

Statistical finding from LMM-I and LMM-II effect contrasts on the iteration predictor:

Initial inferences are significantly slower for specific engine/model combinations:
- **LibTorch:** significantly higher RpS (runtime per sample) for first iterations across all models (p < 0.0001). CNN-29k is worst: first 9 iterations have significantly higher RpS than the average.
- **TensorFlow Lite:** significantly higher only for CNN-29k; tendency toward decreasing RpS after first iterations visible in other models too
- **ONNX Runtime:** HNN-11k shows decreasing tendency; CNN-29k shows alternating (not decreasing) RpS values

The correlation with real-time violations is direct: the same engine/model combinations that show more frequent real-time violations during initial inferences also show significantly longer initial runtimes. Conclusion from authors: "we recommend warm up phases before the audio callback to ensure more reliable runtimes."

Anira's InferenceConfig supports a `warm_up` parameter (number of warm-up inferences before start, default = 0) as first-class configuration.

The benchmarking methodology is designed to capture this: each repetition starts fresh, allowing the warm-up phase behavior to be observed multiple times across repetitions.
