# ddsp-2020

**Full title:** DDSP: Differentiable Digital Signal Processing
**Authors:** Jesse Engel, Lamtharn Hantrakul, Chenjie Gu, Adam Roberts (Google Brain)
**Venue:** ICLR 2020
**arXiv:** 2001.04643
**Original source:** `/Users/morgan/code/murail/.design/references/papers/archive/ddsp-2020.pdf`

## Summary

DDSP integrates classical DSP components (oscillators, filters, reverb) into TensorFlow's automatic differentiation graph, enabling end-to-end training of synthesizers. The core demonstration is a DDSP autoencoder that takes audio, encodes fundamental frequency (F0), loudness, and a residual timbre vector (z), then reconstructs audio via an additive harmonic synthesizer combined with a filtered noise synthesizer. Results: high-fidelity audio synthesis without autoregressive or adversarial losses, models 2-10x smaller than baselines, and interpretable control over pitch, loudness, timbre, and room acoustics.

## Key Contributions

- Harmonic oscillator + filtered noise synthesizer as differentiable modules
- Frequency sampling method for interpretable linear-phase FIR filter design
- Frequency-domain reverb implementation scaling as O(n log n)
- Multi-scale spectral loss for perceptually grounded reconstruction
- Disentangled latent (f0, loudness, z) enabling extrapolation beyond training distribution
