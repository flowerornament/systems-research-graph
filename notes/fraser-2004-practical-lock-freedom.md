# Practical lock-freedom (Fraser 2004)

**Citation:** Keir Fraser. *Practical lock-freedom.* Technical Report UCAM-CL-TR-579, University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory, February 2004. PhD dissertation.

**Abstract summary:** Introduces the first practical lock-free implementations of MCAS (multi-word compare-and-swap) and FSTM (software transactional memory), builds lock-free skip lists, BSTs, and red-black trees on top of these abstractions, and demonstrates through empirical evaluation on a 106-processor Sun Fire server that these implementations match or surpass high-quality lock-based designs.
