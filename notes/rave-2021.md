# rave-2021

**Full title:** RAVE: A Variational Autoencoder for Fast and High-Quality Neural Audio Synthesis
**Authors:** Antoine Caillon & Philippe Esling (IRCAM / Sorbonne Université, CNRS UMR 9912)
**Venue:** arXiv:2111.05011v2, December 2021
**Original source:** `/Users/morgan/code/murail/.design/references/papers/archive/rave-2021.pdf`

## Summary

RAVE introduces a Realtime Audio Variational autoEncoder that achieves 48kHz audio synthesis at 20x faster than real-time on CPU and 240x on GPU. The key architectural contributions are: (1) a two-stage training procedure separating representation learning (spectral loss VAE) from synthesis quality (adversarial decoder fine-tuning); (2) a 16-band PQMF multiband decomposition reducing temporal dimensionality 16x; (3) a post-training SVD analysis for identifying effective latent dimensionality; and (4) a fidelity parameter enabling deployment-time trade-off between compactness and reconstruction quality.

Evaluated against NSynth (MOS 2.68, 64.7M params) and SING (MOS 1.15, 80.8M params), RAVE achieves MOS 3.01 with only 17.6M parameters.
