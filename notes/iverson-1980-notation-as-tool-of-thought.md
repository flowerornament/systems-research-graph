# iverson-1980-notation-as-tool-of-thought

Kenneth E. Iverson, "Notation as a Tool of Thought," *Communications of the ACM*, 23(8), 1980. Originally delivered as the 1979 ACM Turing Award Lecture.

Iverson's thesis: the advantages of executability and universality found in programming languages can be effectively combined, in a single coherent language, with the advantages offered by mathematical notation. APL is the vehicle for this demonstration.

The paper develops this in five sections: (1) characteristics of good notation (ease of expression, suggestivity, subordination of detail, economy, amenability to proof); (2) polynomials; (3) permutations and directed graphs; (4) formal proofs; (5) conclusion comparing APL with mathematical notation, discussing introduction of notation in context, and cautioning against premature efficiency emphasis.

A 1986 postscript surveys progress in APL-based education since the original paper.
